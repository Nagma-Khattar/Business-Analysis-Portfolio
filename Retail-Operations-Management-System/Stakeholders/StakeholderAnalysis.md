# Stakeholder Analysis

## Retail Operations Management System (ROMS)

**Client:** NorthStar Retail Inc.  
**Document Type:** Stakeholder Analysis  
**Prepared By:** Nagma Khattar  

---

# 1. Introduction

The purpose of this document is to identify all stakeholders involved in the Retail Operations Management System (ROMS) project, understand their roles, responsibilities, interests, influence on the project, and define an appropriate communication strategy.

Effective stakeholder management ensures that project requirements are accurately gathered, expectations are managed, and project objectives are successfully achieved.

---

# 2. Stakeholder Register

| Stakeholder | Role | Interest | Influence |
|-------------|------|----------|-----------|
| Project Sponsor | Provides funding and strategic direction | High | High |
| Store Manager | Oversees daily retail operations | High | High |
| Inventory Manager | Manages inventory and stock levels | High | Medium |
| Sales Associate | Records customer sales transactions | High | Low |
| Customer Service Representative | Manages customer profiles and inquiries | Medium | Low |
| System Administrator | Manages users, roles, and system configuration | High | High |
| Business Analyst | Gathers and documents requirements | High | Medium |
| Development Team | Designs and develops the system | High | Medium |
| Quality Assurance Team | Tests the system before deployment | High | Medium |
| End Users | Daily users of the application | High | Low |

---

# 3. Stakeholder Roles and Responsibilities

## Project Sponsor

Responsibilities:

- Approve project funding.
- Approve major project decisions.
- Provide strategic direction.
- Review project progress.

---

## Store Manager

Responsibilities:

- Provide business requirements.
- Validate operational workflows.
- Approve business functionality.
- Support user acceptance testing.

---

## Inventory Manager

Responsibilities:

- Define inventory requirements.
- Validate inventory reports.
- Ensure inventory accuracy.

---

## Sales Associate

Responsibilities:

- Process customer sales.
- Validate sales functionality.
- Provide usability feedback.

---

## Customer Service Representative

Responsibilities:

- Maintain customer information.
- Verify customer management features.
- Support customer-related testing.

---

## System Administrator

Responsibilities:

- Manage users and permissions.
- Configure security settings.
- Monitor system availability.

---

## Business Analyst

Responsibilities:

- Gather business requirements.
- Prepare BRD and FRD.
- Create user stories.
- Facilitate stakeholder communication.
- Support sprint planning activities.

---

## Development Team

Responsibilities:

- Design system components.
- Develop application features.
- Resolve technical issues.
- Support system deployment.

---

## Quality Assurance Team

Responsibilities:

- Prepare test cases.
- Execute functional testing.
- Report defects.
- Verify issue resolution.

---

## End Users

Responsibilities:

- Use the system for daily operations.
- Participate in User Acceptance Testing (UAT).
- Provide feedback for improvements.

# 5. Communication Plan

| Stakeholder | Information Required | Communication Method | Frequency |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Project Sponsor | Project status, risks, budget | Project Status Meeting | Bi-weekly |
| Store Manager | Requirements, progress, issues | Sprint Review | Every Sprint |
| Inventory Manager | Inventory features | Workshops | As Required |
| Development Team | Requirements and user stories | Daily Scrum | Daily |
| QA Team | Functional requirements and acceptance criteria | Sprint Planning | Every Sprint |
| End Users | Training and project updates | Email and User Training | Before Deployment |

---

# 6. Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

To ensure successful implementation of ROMS, stakeholders will be actively engaged throughout the project lifecycle.

The Business Analyst will facilitate regular meetings, workshops, sprint reviews, and feedback sessions to ensure stakeholder expectations are addressed and project objectives remain aligned with business needs.

Continuous communication and collaboration will minimize project risks, improve requirement quality, and encourage user adoption.

---

# 7. Conclusion

The success of the Retail Operations Management System depends on effective stakeholder participation throughout the project lifecycle.

Clearly defining stakeholder roles, responsibilities, communication methods, and engagement strategies will help ensure successful project delivery, user satisfaction, and achievement of business objectives.
